Mahatma Gandhi

Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi, commonly known as Mahatma Gandhi, was an Indian lawyer, anti-colonial nationalist, and political ethicist who employed nonviolent resistance to lead the successful campaign for India's independence from British rule. He inspired movements for civil rights and freedom across the world. The honorific Mahātmā (Sanskrit: "high-souled", "venerable") was first applied to him in 1914 in South Africa – a country where he lived for 21 years, and where he developed his political views, ethics, and political leadership skills.


Subhas Chandra Bose

Subhas Chandra Bose was a great Indian freedom fighter and nationalist leader who played an important role in India’s struggle for independence. Popularly known as “Netaji,” he believed in fighting against British rule through strong action and leadership. He formed the Indian National Army (INA) to fight for freedom. Bose inspired millions with his courage, patriotism, discipline, and dedication to achieving an independent and strong India.
